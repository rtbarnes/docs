## Did you know?

Viggo Mortensen, who played Aragorn in Peter Jackson's *The Lord of the Rings* trilogy, was a last-minute replacement. Stuart Townsend had originally been cast and had trained for several weeks, but was let go just one day before principal photography began in October 1999 because the filmmakers felt he was too young for the role. Mortensen accepted the part largely at the urging of his then 11-year-old son Henry, who was a fan of the books, and flew to New Zealand with almost no preparation.

To stay in character throughout the long shoot, Mortensen carried his sword everywhere — into restaurants, hotels, and even while driving — and personally paid to ship the horse he rode in the films, Uraeus, back to the United States after filming wrapped.
