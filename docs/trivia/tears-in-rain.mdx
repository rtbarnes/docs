## Did you know?

The famous "Tears in Rain" monologue, delivered by Rutger Hauer as the replicant Roy Batty in the film's climax, was largely rewritten by Hauer himself the night before filming.

The original script was longer and more verbose. Hauer trimmed it down and added the now-iconic closing line:

> "All those moments will be lost in time, like tears in rain. Time to die."

When Hauer finished the take on set, the crew reportedly applauded and some were in tears. The monologue is now widely regarded as one of the greatest improvised speeches in film history.
